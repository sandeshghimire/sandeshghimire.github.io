About

I am an embedded firmware engineer working at a stealth mode health care startup at Silicon Valley. I have over a decade of experience working on mission critical embedded systems from automotive system, networking time and synchronization, consumer electronic and medical device.

I have significant experience working with ARM M0, M3 and M4 microcontrollers from wide verity of venders including NXP, Freescale, Atmel, ST Micro, TI and Microchip.

For the last few years I have been working on ARM A9/A15 SoC from Xilinx and Altera. I have single handedly build the board support package, port bootloader (u-boot). Port Linux kernel, write device drivers and applications

Xilinx Zynq 7000


Xilinx All Programmable SoCs (AP SoC) are processor-centric platforms that offer software, hardware and I/O programmability in a single chip. The Zynq-7000 family is based on the All Programmable SoC architecture. Zynq-7000 products incorporate a dual core ARM Cortex-A9 based Processing System (PS) and Xilinx Programmable Logic in a single device.

Altera SoC


Altera SoCs integrate an ARM-based hard processor system (HPS) consisting of processor, peripherals, and memory interfaces with the FPGA fabric using a high-bandwidth interconnect backbone. It combines the performance and power savings of hard intellectual property (IP) with the flexibility of programmable logic.
